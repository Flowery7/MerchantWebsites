3 Reasons You Must Have a Website

Published on 23 May 2025

"I get enough work through word-of-mouth."
Many sole traders rely on referrals—but what if those dry up? A website isn't just a backup plan; it's your most powerful marketing tool. Here's why:

1. Instant Credibility (Before You Even Meet)

Over 80% of customers research a business online before contacting them. If your only presence is a Facebook page or a Gumtree ad, you're losing trust—and clients. In today’s digital-first world, customers expect a website. Without one, your business appears less legitimate, less established, and less professional.

How a website helps:

  • Showcase professional photos of your work – A gallery of your past jobs demonstrates your skills, builds trust, and shows potential clients what to expect. Whether it's plastering, gardening, or tiling, people want to see before-and-after shots.
  • Display testimonials from happy clients – Social proof builds instant trust. Genuine reviews on your site help reassure potential customers they’re making the right choice.
  • List qualifications, insurance, and certifications – Showing you're fully insured or certified (e.g., Gas Safe, NVQ) can be the difference between getting the job and losing it.

2. Be Found Where Clients Are Looking

Google processes 8.5 billion searches daily. That’s where your customers are searching for services like yours. If you're not showing up for searches like "plasterer in Birmingham" or "local cleaner near me," you're invisible to ready-to-buy clients.

Local SEO benefits:

  • Appear in "near me" searches – Over 60% of mobile users look for services near them. With a website, you're more likely to show up in these results, especially with proper local SEO.
  • Rank higher than directories like Checkatrade – Unlike directories that often prioritize paid listings, your own website gives you full control over how you're presented—and helps you stand out.
  • Get calls from ready-to-buy clients – A well-optimized website attracts clients who already want what you offer. They're not just browsing—they're booking.

3. Saves Time (and Filters Bad Clients)

How many hours do you lose explaining your prices, availability, or service area? A website works as your 24/7 assistant, handling common questions and giving clients what they need—fast. Plus, it helps weed out time-wasters who aren't a good fit.

Time-saving features:

  • FAQ page to reduce repetitive calls – Cover common topics like “Do you work weekends?”, “How much do you charge?”, or “What areas do you cover?” so you only hear from serious clients.
  • Online booking or contact forms to organize enquiries – Instead of texts or Facebook messages at 10pm, you get clear, structured information in your inbox.
  • Clear service descriptions to weed out mismatched clients – By listing what you do (and what you don’t), you reduce awkward conversations and wasted quotes.
