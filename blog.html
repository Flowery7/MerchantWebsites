3 Reasons You Must Have a Website

Published on 23 May 2025

"I’ve already got a Facebook page—why would I need a website?"
Social media is great for updates and visibility—but it’s not a substitute for your own professional online presence. A website gives you control, authority, and long-term growth. Here's why you shouldn’t rely on social platforms alone:

1. You Don’t Own Your Social Media Page

Facebook, Instagram, or TikTok could change the rules—or suspend your account—overnight. If that happens, your content, followers, and enquiries could disappear without warning. With a website, you’re in control.

Risks of social-only strategies:

  • Algorithm changes hurt reach – You may have 1,000 followers, but only a fraction actually see your posts unless you pay to boost them.
  • Platform bans and suspensions – Even a minor policy violation can lead to temporary bans or permanent deletion.
  • No backup for enquiries or client trust – If your profile vanishes, so do your reviews, messages, and reputation.

2. A Website Makes a Stronger First Impression

Social media is noisy and cluttered. Your business deserves a clean, focused place to show off your services, personality, and professionalism. A website says, "I take my work seriously."

What your website can do better:

  • Tailored design that reflects your brand – Unlike cookie-cutter profiles, your site can use your colours, logo, and layout.
  • No distractions or competitors – On Facebook, your customers can be distracted by ads or even click on a rival’s page.
  • One clear call to action – Whether it’s “Call Now” or “Request a Quote,” your website guides users directly to take action.

3. Long-Term Visibility & Growth

Social posts disappear down the feed fast. A website, especially one built with SEO in mind, brings in traffic for months or even years. It's your best bet for steady, organic growth—without paying for ads.

Website benefits over time:

  • Pages that rank on Google – A blog post or service page can bring in new clients long after it's published.
  • Professional email addresses – Using you@yourbusiness.co.uk boosts trust far more than a Gmail or Hotmail account.
  • Better analytics – Learn what people search for, how they find you, and what services are most popular—data social media doesn’t always give.

A website doesn’t replace your Facebook or Instagram—it amplifies them. Link your socials, embed your posts, and funnel visitors to your site to book or call you directly.
